ENGW 3302: Section 13
Professor Christens Enos

This document will be a blog post hosted on a scalable hosting service like GitHub Pages so that it can be easily shared and accessed anywhere in the United States. The audience of this post is first responders, and the purpose of this post is to generate awareness and interest regarding the lacking cybersecurity affecting the fleets of emergency services across the nation; the post will conclude with a call to action that advises first responders to leverage their unions, fleet managers, and unique position in society in order to incentivize automakers to prioritize cybersecurity in their vehicles. The audience of first responders may not be aware of the lacking cybersecurity affecting their fleets because most are likely not knowledgeable in the field of cybersecurity and because companies like Ford and Fiat Chrysler Automobiles (FCA) put effort to mask cybersecurity vulnerabilities from the public. This post utilizes pathos by leveraging the sense of national pride and desire for safety that many first responders share to highlight why the topic of lacking cybersecurity in first responders’ fleets is so important, both to them and to the United States as a whole. The goal of this post is to provide an easily understandable source of information that first responders can share amongst themselves to raise awareness to evoke the implementation of more effective cybersecurity in their fleets; this will have many positive side-effects for the public such as stimulating the national economy and the possibility of translating similarly effective cybersecurity to consumer vehicles.
